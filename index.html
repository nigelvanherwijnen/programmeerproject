Introduction

Greenhouse gasses and the Earth's atmosphere

Since the industrial revolution, the concentration of greenhouse gasses (GHG) in the Earth’s atmosphere has spiked. As countries continue to thrive, the need for energy is higher than it has ever been. This, among other things, result in the burning of carbon-based fuels such as coal, oil and natural gas. The anthropogenic emissions that come as a side product from these combustions have a devastating impact on the environment.

To prevent the decline of the world’s atmosphere, it is naturally important to know where the problem has to be addressed. In the following graphs, you will be able to see where in the world the most harmful gasses are being emitted and in which sector these gasses originate. Data is available from 1990 up to and including 2012. Striking are milestones in a countries history that are visible in the emission of greenhouse gasses in that time period. Even as some countries succeed to lower their emission of greenhouse gasses, it is visible that the worldwide trend is still increasing.
